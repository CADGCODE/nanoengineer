Extrude Mode

A chunk must be selected in order to enter Extrude mode. The selected chunk will have three versions of itself when entering extrude mode. The white alignment spheres (or Bond-offset spheres) represent the different bond orientations that can be achieved with the extrude tool. By selecting the different spheres the three chunks will align themselves according to the chosen alignment sphere. When two open bonds are close enough to bond their colors will shift from their red default color, to a green and blue color (a green and blue shared sphere represents the best formed bonds). The green and blue colored open bonds indicate that the two open bonds will bond when the done icon is selected. There is a pink sphere in the center of the Bond Offset Spheres. This pink sphere enables the user to move the Bond Offset Sphere’s to a different position.

The radio buttons on the dashboard effect the display of the chunk in extrude mode. “Show” is a category of radio buttons that refers to what is seen in extrude mode. These buttons enable the user to turn the alignment spheres on and off. The “when done” category of radio buttons allows you to choose whether to make bonds and merge parts when reentering selection mode. The number of copies is determined by the N spinbox located on the dashboard. The user can alter the distance between copies by changing the x, y, and z values on the dashboard.

The extrude mode has the ability to extrude in the shape of a rod or a ring. Alpha users should be aware that there is a bug affecting the use of the ring mode in extrude. In the Alpha version align the open bonds to bond in rod mode. Once the green and blue lights are active you can switch to Ring mode. Once you are in ring mode you can determine how many copies of the chunk you wish to have in your ring. Selecting the done icon with green and blue lights present will create a bonded ring. The bug is affecting the chunks ability to create bonded open bonds in ring mode. As long as the bonds are initiated with rod mode, and then the user switches to ring mode the bonding will occur.
