Open Bond

An open bond is a place where one electron is looking for a second electron to pair with. In nanoENGINEER-1™ we call this an open bond. In nanoENGINEER-1™ open bonds are represented by small red spheres.

 

In this image we see one carbon atom. In referencing the periodic table we can deduce that a carbon atom has 4 valence electrons in the outer shell. In nanoENGINEER-1™ these valence electrons are the red spherical caps shown here in tube mode. The valence electrons that remain unbonded are what is referred to as open bonds.
